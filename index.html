My research offers tactics for making AI and biometric systems more ethical and inclusive, by leveraging uniquely human strengths of emotion, embodiment, and imagination.

As an assistant professor at Georgia Tech, I direct the Future Feelings Lab.

Projects

Fabulation for Design Futuring
Envisioning alternative futures and desirable worlds is a core element of design that must be cultivated, especially when a deep transition of practices, values, and power is necessary for vibrant and just future lifeworlds.
Polyvocal Perspectives for Design Futuring
This un-manifesto calls for greater diversity in approaches for design futuring and offers five idiosyncratic approaches drawing from our own personal, cultural, and professional backgrounds.
Expanding Modes of Reflection for Design Futuring
We offer modes of reflection to help design researchers deepen their practice with design futuring: designerly formgiving, temporal representations, positionality and privilege, public engagement, and anticipatory phenomena.
Life-Affirming Biosensing with the Heart Sounds Bench
The Heart Sounds Bench amplifies live unfiltered heart sounds. It resists the colonial impulse to make people transparent via data, instead inviting people to appreciate the live presence and unknowable complexity of one another.
PREMIER: Performance Residencies in Electronic Music for Interdisciplinary Education Research
PREMIER was a series of artist residencies that brought interdisciplinary STEAM education to Georgia Tech students and the Atlanta community with participatory music, arts, and performance opportunities incorporating audio technology and electronics in service of creative practice.
Embodied Transductions through Musical Practice
Embodied Transductions explores alternative ways of knowing bodies, both human and non-human, through hacking biomedical technology for musical expression.
Feeling Air: Exploring Aesthetic and Material Qualities of Architectural Inflatables
These large-scale inflatable sculptures explore material, aesthetic, and somatic qualities of interactive inflatable structures.
Infrastructural Membranes
This inflatable sculpture explores take-for-granted infrastructures of data, air, and feeling.
Salaam Participatory Peace Sculpture
This sculpture is inspired by the Arabic word salaam, used as a greeting to wish peace upon others. The sculpture responds to mounting Islamophobia in the US with a strong, tall form that is at the same time peaceful, gentle, and inviting.
Emotional Biosensing: Exploring Critical Alternatives
Emotion AI predicts emotions and other psychological characteristics from biodata. This project helps chart dominant trends driving technological innovation in Emotion AI, critique ethical harms emerging in this space, and offer alternative approaches for Emotion AI to more respectfully support diverse ways of living good lives.
Color-Changing Garments for Emotional Reflection
This project explores, how does emotional biodata get embroiled in questions of how or what we should feel or be? This design leverages ambiguity to display emotional predictions in a way that invites people to make their own interpretations. Still, tensions arose when people invested too much authority in the technology.
Speculating Near-Future Menstrual Biosensing
We created a set of speculative designs exploring the future of menstrual biosensing technologies for surveillance at home and work.
Cherishing Laughter as Biosensory Data
We present preliminary design explorations of tangible representations of laughter and discuss interaction design opportunities for celebration, cherishing, and reflection.
Color-Changing Fabric as Realtime Data Display
This project explores using dynamic color-changing fabric as a novel kind of data display that invites different ways of engaging with data in everyday life.
Heart Sounds in Cheap Plastic Buckets
The Heart Sounds Buckets explore an uncomfortable contrast of hearing the live unfiltered heart sounds of gallery visitors, as precious and intimate, contrasted with their emanation from cheap plastic buckets, a cold sterile material, to invite reflection on biomedicalization of everyday life.
Performing VR Together
This short participatory performance reimagined virtual reality centered around collective local embodied presence.
Intel Connect Anything Prototyping Kit
This prototyping kit enables using the Intel Galileo microcontroller through GUI programming.
WaaZam!
WaaZam supports shared creative play at a distance.
Defamiliarizing Sound with Granular Synthesis
Blending textures of familiar sounds from everyday life to explore new sonic textures.
Reading NeurIPS Paper Titles
Reading the titles of papers published in advances in Neural Information Processing Systems from 2019, as a performative attempt to distill efforts to stay abreast of rapidly evolving research landscapes.
Myo-Controlled DJ Effects
We made an effects controller that uses the Myo armband, so arm movements can be used to adjust audio effects in the audio software of choice.
Harmonograph Visualizer

Music & Data at The Echo Nest
As a software engineer at small music data startup The Echo Nest, I worked on a variety of data-driven web applications. The Echo Nest was later bought by Spotify.
