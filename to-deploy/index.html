Lissajous curves are a way to visualize stereo sound. The left speaker position is plotted on the horizontal axis, and the right speaker position is plotted on the vertical axis. For simple waveforms, this creates recognizable and often beautiful patterns. Matt and I made a simple app to play with these, using sound, drawings, and interactive controls.

How can we help illiterate Moroccans use mobile phones? They already own at least one, but rely on family, friends, or paying strangers to rifle through their contacts' information and make calls. They need to record names and numbers, find them again, and dial the number. As a team of Olin design and engineering students, Babson business students, and Moroccan École Nationale de L'Industrie Minérale engineering students, we designed, prototyped, and pilot tested a simple, affordable solution with promising results. more
